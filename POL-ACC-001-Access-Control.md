## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes governance for managing access to CodeVolt information assets to ensure appropriate protection against unauthorized access.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All user access to information systems
- All types of users (employees, contractors, third parties)
- All access methods (local, remote, network)
- All information processing facilities

---

## 3. Governance

### Policy Owner
The Information Security Manager owns this policy and is responsible for:
- Ensuring alignment with business requirements
- Reviewing access control effectiveness
- Reporting on compliance to senior management

### Implementation
System administrators implement technical controls in accordance with this policy.

---

## 4. Access Control Principles

### Least Privilege
Users are granted the minimum access rights necessary to perform their duties.

### Need to Know
Access is granted based on legitimate business requirements and job functions.

### Segregation of Duties
Conflicting duties are separated to prevent unauthorized actions.

### Regular Review
Access rights are reviewed periodically and upon role changes.

---

## 5. User Access Management

### Access Provisioning
- Access requests require appropriate authorization
- Rights granted based on role and business need
- Users acknowledge acceptable use obligations
- Access documented and tracked

### Access Review
- Periodic review of user access rights
- Immediate review upon role change
- Verification of continued business need

### Access Revocation
- Timely revocation when no longer required
- Immediate revocation upon termination
- Immediate revocation for security violations

---

## 6. Authentication

### Password Requirements
Authentication mechanisms enforce:
- Minimum password complexity
- Appropriate password length
- Regular rotation or event-based change
- Protection against reuse

### Multi-Factor Authentication
Sensitive systems and functions require multi-factor authentication commensurate with risk.

### Authentication Failure Handling
Repeated authentication failures result in:
- Account lockout or delay
- Security alert generation
- Investigation of potential attacks

---

## 7. Privileged Access

Privileged access is subject to:
- Enhanced authorization requirements
- Additional monitoring and logging
- Just-in-time access where practicable
- Regular review of necessity

Privileged accounts are:
- Separate from standard user accounts
- Used only for authorized administrative activities
- Subject to additional oversight

---

## 8. Review

This policy is reviewed annually or upon significant change to:
- Business operations
- Technology infrastructure
- Threat landscape
- Regulatory requirements

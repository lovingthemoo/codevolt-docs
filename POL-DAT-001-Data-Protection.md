## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes governance for the protection of personal data throughout its lifecycle in compliance with data protection legislation and organizational requirements.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all processing of personal data by CodeVolt, including:
- Collection, storage, and use of personal data
- Data sharing and transfers
- Data retention and disposal
- Data subject rights management

---

## 3. Governance

### Policy Owner
The Information Security Manager owns this policy and is responsible for:
- Ensuring compliance with data protection requirements
- Maintaining data protection procedures
- Reporting on compliance to senior management

### Data Protection Principles
Personal data shall be:
- Processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently
- Collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes
- Adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary
- Accurate and kept up to date
- Retained only as long as necessary
- Processed securely with appropriate measures

---

## 4. Lawful Processing

Processing of personal data is based on:
- Consent of the data subject where appropriate
- Contractual necessity for service delivery
- Legal obligations requiring processing
- Legitimate interests (balanced against individual rights)

---

## 5. Data Subject Rights

CodeVolt respects and facilitates the exercise of data subject rights:

### Rights Under GDPR
- **Right to be informed** — Clear privacy notices provided
- **Right of access** — Subject access requests processed
- **Right to rectification** — Data accuracy maintained
- **Right to erasure** — Deletion requests honored where applicable
- **Right to restrict processing** — Limitations applied as requested
- **Right to data portability** — Data provided in structured formats
- **Right to object** — Objections to processing considered

### Exercising Rights
Data subjects may exercise rights by contacting rook@codevolt.co.uk. Responses are provided within statutory timeframes.

---

## 6. Data Security

Appropriate security measures protect personal data:
- Encryption for data at rest and in transit
- Access controls limiting data access
- Regular security assessments
- Incident detection and response capabilities

---

## 7. Data Retention

Personal data is retained only as long as necessary for:
- The purposes for which it was collected
- Legal and regulatory requirements
- Legitimate business needs

Retention periods are defined and reviewed periodically. Data is securely disposed of when no longer required.

---

## 8. Data Transfers

Transfers of personal data outside the UK/EU are subject to:
- Assessment of adequacy of protection
- Appropriate safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses)
- Documentation of transfer mechanisms

---

## 9. Breach Notification

Personal data breaches are subject to:
- Immediate assessment and containment
- Notification to supervisory authorities where required
- Communication to affected data subjects where high risk
- Documentation and remediation

---

## 10. Review

This policy is reviewed:
- At least annually
- Upon changes to data protection law
- Following significant data incidents
- When processing activities change materially

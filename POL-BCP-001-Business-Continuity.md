## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes governance for maintaining business continuity and recovering from disruptive incidents to ensure resilience of critical operations.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- Business continuity planning and management
- Disaster recovery capabilities
- Resumption of critical business processes
- Recovery of information systems and data

---

## 3. Governance

### Business Continuity Owner
Senior management ensures business continuity capabilities are appropriate for organizational needs.

The Information Security Manager coordinates:
- Business continuity procedures
- Recovery capability maintenance
- Testing and exercise programs
- Management reporting

---

## 4. Business Impact Analysis

Critical business processes are identified and analysed to determine:
- Recovery Time Objectives (RTO)
- Recovery Point Objectives (RPO)
- Resource requirements for recovery
- Interdependencies between processes

Analysis is reviewed periodically and when business processes change.

---

## 5. Continuity Strategies

Strategies are established for:
- Maintaining essential operations during disruption
- Recovery of systems and data within defined timeframes
- Alternative processing capabilities where required
- Communication during business interruptions

---

## 6. Backup and Recovery

### Backup Requirements
- Critical data is backed up according to defined schedules
- Backup integrity is verified
- Recovery procedures are documented
- Backup media is protected commensurate with data sensitivity

### Recovery Procedures
- Documented procedures for system restoration
- Defined roles and responsibilities
- Communication plans during recovery
- Escalation procedures

---

## 7. Testing and Exercises

Business continuity capabilities are validated through:
- Regular testing of backup restoration
- Exercise of recovery procedures
- Review of test results and improvements
- Updates based on lessons learned

Testing is conducted without disrupting production operations where possible.

---

## 8. Review

This policy and associated procedures are reviewed:
- At least annually
- Following significant changes to business processes
- After tests or actual recovery events

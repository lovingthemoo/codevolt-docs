## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes governance for the identification, assessment, and response to information security incidents to minimize impact and prevent recurrence.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All information security incidents affecting CodeVolt assets
- Incident detection, reporting, and response
- Post-incident activities and learning

---

## 3. Governance

### Incident Response Team
Senior management defines incident response roles and responsibilities.

The Information Security Manager coordinates:
- Incident response procedures
- Communication during incidents
- Post-incident review
- Reporting to management

---

## 4. Incident Classification

Incidents are classified by severity to prioritize response:

| Classification | Criteria | Response Priority |
|---------------|----------|------------------|
| **Critical** | Data breach or system compromise | Immediate |
| **High** | Significant service disruption | Urgent |
| **Medium** | Moderate impact or suspicious activity | Normal |
| **Low** | Minimal impact or policy violation | Standard |

Classification considers:
- Impact on business operations
- Sensitivity of affected information
- Scope of exposure
- Regulatory implications

---

## 5. Incident Response Process

### Detection and Reporting
- Automated monitoring and alerting
- User reporting mechanisms
- Third-party notifications
- All incidents reported to rook@codevolt.co.uk

### Assessment and Classification
- Verify incident validity
- Determine scope and impact
- Classify severity
- Assign response resources

### Containment
- Limit immediate damage
- Preserve evidence
- Maintain business continuity where possible

### Eradication and Recovery
- Remove threat causes
- Restore affected systems
- Verify integrity before return to operation

### Post-Incident Activities
- Document lessons learned
- Update procedures based on findings
- Communicate to relevant stakeholders

---

## 6. Communication

Incident communication follows these principles:
- **Internal:** Timely notification to management
- **Affected Parties:** Notification when personal data involved
- **Regulatory:** Disclosure as required by law
- **Public:** Only when necessary and through approved channels

---

## 7. Evidence Preservation

Evidence relevant to incidents is:
- Preserved for investigation
- Maintained securely
- Documented with chain of custody
- Retained per defined periods

---

## 8. Review

This policy is reviewed:
- At least annually
- Following significant incidents
- When procedures change materially

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the governance framework for identifying, classifying, and managing organizational assets to ensure appropriate protection throughout their lifecycle.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all assets that support CodeVolt's information security management system, including:
- Information and data assets
- Software and systems
- Infrastructure and services
- Personnel and competencies

---

## 3. Governance and Accountability

### Roles and Responsibilities

**Senior Management**
- Provide resources for asset management
- Approve asset classification framework
- Review asset management effectiveness

**Information Security Manager**
- Maintain asset inventory governance
- Oversee classification scheme
- Monitor compliance with this policy

**Asset Owners**
- Identify and classify assets within their domain
- Determine appropriate protection requirements
- Review asset classifications periodically

**All Personnel**
- Handle assets in accordance with classification
- Report asset changes or incidents
- Participate in asset management processes

---

## 4. Asset Identification

All assets material to the information security management system shall be identified and recorded. The asset inventory shall be maintained to reflect current organizational operations.

Asset categories include:
- **Information assets** — Data, databases, documentation
- **Software assets** — Applications, operating systems, utilities
- **Physical assets** — Hardware, infrastructure, facilities
- **Service assets** — Cloud services, hosted platforms, third-party services
- **Human assets** — Skills, competencies, roles

---

## 5. Asset Classification

Assets shall be classified based on sensitivity and criticality to the organization.

### Classification Levels

| Classification | Criteria | Protection Requirements |
|---------------|----------|------------------------|
| **Public** | Intended for public disclosure | Standard handling |
| **Internal** | Organization use only | Access controls |
| **Confidential** | Sensitive business or customer information | Enhanced controls, encryption |
| **Restricted** | Critical to operations or security | Maximum controls, limited access |

### Classification Principles
- Classification determined by asset owner
- Based on potential impact of unauthorized disclosure, modification, or loss
- Reviewed periodically and when circumstances change
- Documented in asset inventory

---

## 6. Protection Requirements

Protection measures shall be proportional to classification and risk assessment.

### General Requirements
- Assets protected commensurate with classification
- Access restricted to authorized personnel
- Handling requirements communicated to users
- Asset ownership clearly assigned

### Confidential and Restricted Assets
- Encryption for storage and transmission
- Access logging and monitoring
- Enhanced authentication where appropriate
- Regular review of access rights

---

## 7. Asset Lifecycle

### Acquisition
- Security requirements defined prior to acquisition
- Risk assessment for new asset types
- Classification determined at acquisition

### Operation
- Protection measures implemented and maintained
- Regular review of classification and controls
- Incident reporting for security events

### Disposal
- Secure disposal procedures for all asset types
- Verification of data destruction
- Documentation of disposal activities

---

## 8. Review and Maintenance

- Asset inventory reviewed at least annually
- Classifications updated when circumstances change
- Policy effectiveness assessed during management reviews
- Improvements implemented as identified

---

## 9. Compliance

Non-compliance with this policy may result in:
- Review of asset access rights
- Remedial training
- Disciplinary action where appropriate

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the governance framework for information security risk management to ensure risks are identified, assessed, and treated systematically.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All information security risks to organizational assets
- Risk assessment and treatment activities
- Risk acceptance decisions
- Ongoing risk monitoring and review

---

## 3. Governance

### Risk Owner
Senior management owns information security risk and is responsible for:
- Ensuring risk management is integrated into business processes
- Accepting residual risks
- Providing resources for risk treatment
- Reviewing risk management effectiveness

### Risk Management Coordinator
The Information Security Manager coordinates:
- Risk assessment processes
- Risk register maintenance
- Risk reporting to management
- Treatment plan monitoring

---

## 4. Risk Assessment Methodology

### Risk Identification
Risks are identified through:
- Systematic analysis of assets, threats, and vulnerabilities
- Consideration of internal and external factors
- Input from relevant stakeholders
- Review of security incidents and trends

### Risk Analysis
Risk analysis evaluates:
- Likelihood of risk occurrence
- Potential impact on the organization
- Effectiveness of existing controls
- Residual risk after controls

### Risk Evaluation
Risks are evaluated against risk criteria established by senior management to determine priority for treatment.

---

## 5. Risk Treatment

### Treatment Options
Risks may be treated by:
- **Mitigation** — Implementing controls to reduce risk
- **Transfer** — Sharing risk with third parties (e.g., insurance)
- **Acceptance** — Acknowledging and monitoring risk
- **Avoidance** — Discontinuing activities that create risk

### Control Selection
Controls are selected based on:
- Cost-benefit analysis
- Effectiveness in reducing risk
- Alignment with organizational capabilities
- Compliance requirements

### Residual Risk
Residual risks are evaluated and accepted by appropriate management levels.

---

## 6. Risk Monitoring and Review

### Ongoing Monitoring
The risk landscape is monitored through:
- Regular review of risk indicators
- Incident analysis
- Changes in threat environment
- Operational experience

### Review Schedule
- **Monthly:** Review of critical and high risks
- **Quarterly:** Comprehensive risk register review
- **Annually:** Full risk assessment refresh
- **Upon change:** When business or threat landscape changes

---

## 7. Risk Communication

Risk information is communicated to:
- Senior management for strategic decisions
- System owners for operational management
- All personnel for awareness and compliance

---

## 8. Review

This policy is reviewed annually or upon significant organizational or environmental change.
